Factions of the Forsarr War

Learn about the various forces vying for control of the Forsarr System

Faction Overview

SH
368th & 426th Cadian Mechanized Super Heavy Assault Reconnaissance Reserve

Embodying the grit and unyielding resolve of the Astra Militarum, these elite Cadian units are known for deploying Baneblades and other super-heavy armor in aggressive, overwhelming spearhead assaults. At Druscar's Ruin, they fought with brutal efficiency but were eventually overwhelmed, leaving behind wrecked tanks and defiant last stands.

Theme: Sacrifice, perseverance under fire, and the grim futility of loyalty in a collapsing warzone.
Astra Militarum
GS
Garnet Swans (Space Marines)

An elite Astartes Chapter renowned for aerial assault and precision jump-pack strikes. Led by the heroic Captain Kaghutai Khan, they excelled in hit-and-run warfare but suffered catastrophic losses in their final stand against a Gorkanaut.

Theme: Heroic defiance and tragic nobility; the angels of death fighting against impossible odds with grace and fury.
Space Marines
RS
Red Skulls (Space Marines)

Mysterious and relentless, the Red Skulls emerged later in the campaign during the Battle for Druscar's Throne. Fierce warriors who fought to reclaim what they believed to be a lost Imperial command relic, they were ultimately consumed by greater forces beyond mortal comprehension.

Theme: Zealous reclaimers seeking redemption and power in the face of unknown terror.
Space Marines
201
201st Cadian Ordnance Battalion

A disciplined and determined force focused on reclaiming the Forsarr Sub-Sector through superior firepower and structured defense. They served as the Imperium's strategic backbone in the battle for Druscar's Throne but were decimated by Chaos and the awakening C'tan.

Theme: Rational command meets arcane horror; precision undone by cosmic chaos.
Astra Militarum
M9
Adeptus Mechanicus – Cohort Omnis-9

Once the dominant Tech-priesthood in the sector, this Mechanicus force fractured during the Kelthorn Heresy. Their collapse into civil war was triggered by the Sensory Nexus, leading to horrific internal conflict and eventual corruption.

Theme: The peril of knowledge and the fragility of faith; logic undone by the lure of sensation and heresy.
Mechanicus
DG
Death Guard – Destroyer Hive Plague Company

Vile and relentless, this company spreads decay like a creeping doom. In both Druscar's Throne and across Zanthis III, they seek not only to destroy but to transform the very fabric of reality into diseased ruin.

Theme: The inexorability of rot; entropy with a purpose, corrupting all that lives.
Death Guard
Daemons of Slaanesh & Nurgle – Hosts of Excess and Decay

Syll'Esske and their daemonic hosts represent the dual horrors of sensory overload and rot. Drawn to Druscar against their will by an ancient signal, they were ultimately consumed or erased by a force they could not comprehend.

Theme: Cosmic arrogance punished; even the warp is not immune to forgotten truths.
Daemons
DM
Dark Mechanicum – Archmagos Diabolus Arcturus

Once a loyal Tech-Magos, Arcturus was seduced by the Nexus' whispers. Now a blasphemous fusion of machine and daemon, he commands warp-infused forges on Kelthorn II, spreading his techno-daemonic empire through daemon engines and twisted Skitarii.

Theme: Blasphemous innovation; the dark promise of merging flesh, machine, and madness.
Dark Mechanicum
ORK
Orks – Bogggoff's Goff Goggaz (and Reborn Waaagh!)

Brutal, direct, and hilarious in their chaos, Bogggoff's warband was pivotal in Druscar's fall. Even after his death, the Orks surged on in anarchic waves. In the later battle for Druscar's Throne, they returned, hungry for loot, war, and possibly a new crown.

Theme: Unstoppable brutality fueled by sheer enthusiasm for war and challenge.
Orks
TYR
Tyranids – Hive Fleet Leviathan (Incursion roh-epsilon-245)

Alien and unrelenting, the Tyranids devastated Druscar before inexplicably vanishing. Something older than the Hive Mind called them away, leaving behind husks and a void of instinct.

Theme: The predator becomes prey; even perfect evolution bows to deeper terror.
Tyranids
CT
The Forgotten Tyrant – Starborn Horror (C'tan Shard)

A primordial god-entity sealed beneath Druscar, the so-called "Throne" was not power—it was prison. Once freed, this fragment of a C'tan, older than time, disrupted reality, erased daemons, and turned the battlefield into a maelstrom of impossible power.

Theme: Cosmic reckoning; the unearthing of truths no race should awaken.
Ancient Power
